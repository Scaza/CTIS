Team Member 1 Tutone Scott 21136820 After thoroughly researching this topic and viewing arguments from both sides, I really think that it would be in everyone's best interest if we can integrate this technology into society. This is reflected in the eagerness of some institutions to implement this technology within their structures. In police enforcement the potential amount of crime that can be prevented by catching criminal individuals is massive and would be a major benefit to their respective communities. In saying that it also showed me the massive potential for misuse and abuse if not implemented correctly. The arguments regarding its invasion of privacy rights and potential use by law enforcement institutions to target people on certain characteristics is not unfounded. As mentioned in the other pages of the website, there can be inaccuracies when it comes to identifying people of different ethnicities, which can possibly lead to perpetuation of stereotypes and discrimination. In America for example it was noted that facial recognition systems were more likely to mis-identify or fail to identify African American citizens than any other race. This can lead to unjust situations and misrepresentation of arrests made based on the technology that is being used. For me facial recognition has a long way to go and lots of things need to be carefully considered if we want society to enjoy the potential benefits that this technology has to offer.
